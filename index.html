Target Audience

The target audience here is women from the ages of 40-65. Or gay men from the age of 24-50. The income of this target audience is going to be fairly high because French Bulldogs are one of the most expensive kind of dogs to buy. They are small and cute and even though they are too big to go in a purse which younger women love to do, they are small enough to be enticing to these women and men. They probably live in urban environments and are fairly social in their lives.

Client Goals | User Needs

The goal of the Client is to portray information about peoples pets. They do this by showcasing certain Vets. The vets are able to advertise on the site. Advertising is how this site gets their money. They bring the user in and then they give them options on how to accomplish their goals, which in the end are taking good care of their dogs. The user is searching for information about how they can be better pet owners. The fine line here is giving enough information that the user is satisfied with the reliability of information, while at the same time encouraging the process of hooking up with one of the vets on the site. They also advertise for groomers and pet carriers. "I trust this site, so I'm going to use their people."

Design Critique

This is a veterinary website and they want you to feel safe about leaving your pet with them. They chose the color green for the background color because it symbolizes safety, harmony, growth. Green has a healing power and what better feeling to get when you're looking for a hospital for your pet than healing? It's a good thing that they stayed away from yellow-green because it puts forth a feeling of sickness and jealousy. They used a kind of childish serif font which manages to put forth a feeling of reliability, while at the same time making them seem approachable. Then there is a picture of the animals they work with. The picture is a cute image of some French Bulldog Puppies which continues to tell you who they are and grabs your attention.
